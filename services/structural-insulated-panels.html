Structual Insulated Panels

Keep Your Timber Framed Home Warm

Structural Insulated Panels (SIPs) are high-performance building panels made of an insulating foam core sandwiched between two structural facings. Known for superior energy efficiency, strength, and speed of construction, SIPs create airtight, well-insulated homes and buildings. They reduce energy costs, improve indoor comfort, and offer a sustainable alternative to traditional framing methods.

How do SIPs work?

Structural Insulated Panels (SIPs) are high-performance building panels made of an insulating foam core bonded between two structural facings, typically oriented strand board (OSB). They form a strong, airtight, and energy-efficient building envelope when joined together.

What are the benefits of SIPS?

SIPs offer superior insulation, faster construction times, and improved structural strength. They help create airtight homes that reduce drafts, enhancing comfort and energy efficiency.

Are SIPs more energy-efficient than traditional insulation?

Yes, SIPs are significantly more energy-efficient than traditional insulation methods. Their continuous insulation design signifigantly minimizes thermal bridging, reducing heat loss and lowering energy bills.

How do SIPs impact the overall cost?

While SIPs may have a higher upfront cost compared to traditional framing and insulating methods, they often reduce labor costs due to faster installation. Additionally, their energy efficiency can also lead to long-term savings on heating and cooling, making them a smart investment.
